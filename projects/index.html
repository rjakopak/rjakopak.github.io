Where does ungulate migration come from?

Seasonal migration is a behavior where animals move between different ranges throughout the year in a way that lets them reap benefits in both places. Ungulates in the United States often migrate between low-elevation winter ranges and high-elevation summer ranges. The winter ranges are more hospitable during the snowy, cold winters, and summer ranges have lush vegetation, but would be unlivable in the winter.
